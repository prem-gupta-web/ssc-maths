ARITHMETIC

Essential mathematical operations used in everyday calculations and business mathematics. These fundamental concepts form the building blocks for more advanced mathematical studies.

Popular

Percentage

Calculating parts per hundred and their applications in finance, statistics, and everyday life.

Percentage = (Part/Whole) × 100
Percentage Change = (New - Original)/Original × 100
Class Sheet Notes Practice

Profit, Loss & Discount

Business mathematics concepts for financial calculations including markups, markdowns, and sales pricing.

Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price
Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price
Discount = Marked Price - Selling Price
Class Sheet Notes

Average

Central value of a data set calculated by sum of values divided by count, with applications in statistics.

Average = (Sum of terms)/(Number of terms)
Weighted Average = Σ(weight × value)/Σweights
Class Sheet
Popular

Age Problems

Solving problems related to ages of people at different times using linear equations.

If A is x years older than B, and B is y years old now:
A's age = y + x
Ratio of ages = (y + x)/(y)
Class Sheet Practice

Ratio & Proportion

Comparative relationship between quantities and solving problems using proportional relationships.

a:b = c:d ⇒ a×d = b×c
Compound Ratio = (a×c):(b×d)
Class Sheet Notes

Partnership

Business arrangements where partners share profits and losses based on investment ratios.

Profit share ratio = Investment × Time period
Capital Ratio = (Investment₁ × Time₁):(Investment₂ × Time₂)

Mixture & Alligation

Techniques for solving mixture problems and weighted averages in chemistry and finance.

Alligation rule: (Cheaper quantity)/(Dearer quantity) = (d - m)/(m - c)
where m = mean price, d = dearer price, c = cheaper price
Popular

Simple & Compound Interest

Calculating interest on loans and investments with different compounding periods.

SI = P×R×T/100
CI = P(1+R/100)n-P
A = P(1 + r/n)nt
Class Sheet Notes Practice

Time & Work

Problems involving work rates and time management for project planning.

Work = Rate × Time
Combined work rate = Sum of individual rates
Efficiency Ratio = Work₁:Work₂

Pipe & Cistern

Problems related to filling and emptying tanks with multiple inlets and outlets.

Net work done = Sum of filling rates - Sum of emptying rates
Time = 1/(Net Rate)
Popular

Time, Speed & Distance

Relationship between motion parameters with applications in physics and transportation.

Speed = Distance/Time
Average speed = Total distance/Total time
Relative speed = Sum/Difference of speeds
Class Sheet Practice

Boats & Streams

Problems involving boats moving with and against currents in rivers and streams.

Downstream speed = Boat speed + Stream speed
Upstream speed = Boat speed - Stream speed
Boat speed = (Downstream + Upstream)/2

ADVANCED MATHS

Higher-level mathematical concepts for academic and competitive examinations. These topics build upon arithmetic fundamentals to solve more complex problems.

Number System

Classification and properties of numbers (integers, rationals, primes) and their applications.

Even: 2n, Odd: 2n+1
Prime: divisible only by 1 and itself
GCD × LCM = Product of numbers
Popular

LCM & HCF

Least common multiples and highest common factors with applications in fraction simplification.

LCM × HCF = Product of numbers
HCF of fractions = HCF of numerators/LCM of denominators
LCM of fractions = LCM of numerators/HCF of denominators
Class Sheet Notes Practice

Simplification

Techniques for simplifying complex expressions using algebraic identities.

BODMAS rule: Brackets, Orders, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction
(a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
(a-b)² = a² - 2ab + b²
Popular

Algebra

Study of mathematical symbols and rules for manipulating equations and expressions.

Quadratic: ax² + bx + c = 0
x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)]/2a
(a+b)³ = a³ + b³ + 3ab(a+b)
Class Sheet Practice

Trigonometry

Relationships between angles and sides of triangles with periodic functions.

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
sin(A+B) = sinAcosB + cosAsinB

Height & Distance

Applications of trigonometry to measure heights and distances in surveying and navigation.

tanθ = Opposite/Adjacent
Angle of elevation: Looking up
Angle of depression: Looking down
Popular

Geometry

Properties and relations of points, lines, surfaces, and solids in Euclidean space.

Sum of interior angles = (n-2)×180°
Pythagoras: a² + b² = c²
Area of triangle = ½ × base × height
Class Sheet Notes

Mensuration (2D & 3D)

Measurement of geometric figures and their parameters like area, volume, and surface area.

Circle: A=πr², C=2πr
Sphere: V=(4/3)πr³, A=4πr²
Cylinder: V=πr²h, A=2πr(h+r)

Coordinate Geometry

Algebraic representation of geometric figures using coordinate systems.

Distance = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²]
Midpoint = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Slope m = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)

Statistics

Collection, analysis, interpretation of numerical data for decision making.

Mean = Σx/n
Median = Middle value
Mode = Most frequent value
Variance = Σ(x-μ)²/n
Popular

Probability

Likelihood of events occurring, fundamental for risk assessment and predictions.

P(E) = n(E)/n(S)
P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A∩B)
P(A|B) = P(A∩B)/P(B)
Class Sheet Practice

Data Interpretation

Analyzing and making sense of data presented in tables, graphs, and charts.

Percentage change = (New-Old)/Old×100
Growth Rate = (Final-Initial)/Initial×100
Average = ΣValues/Count

KEY FORMULAS CHEAT SHEET

Important mathematical formulas for quick reference. Bookmark this section for exam preparation and problem-solving.

Quadratic Equation

General form: ax² + bx + c = 0
Solutions: x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a
Discriminant D = b² - 4ac
Sum of roots = -b/a
Product of roots = c/a

Pythagorean Theorem

For right-angled triangles:
a² + b² = c²
where c is hypotenuse
Special triangles:
30-60-90: 1:√3:2
45-45-90: 1:1:√2

Area of Triangle

A = ½ × base × height
Heron's formula: √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)]
where s = (a+b+c)/2
Using trig: A = ½ab sinC
Equilateral: (√3/4)a²

Compound Interest

A = P(1 + r/n)nt
Where:
A = Final Amount
P = Principal
r = Annual rate
n = Compounds per year
t = Time in years

Distance Formula

2D: d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²]
3D: d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)² + (z₂-z₁)²]
Slope m = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)
Midpoint = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)

Trig Identities

Pythagorean:
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ
Angle Sum:
sin(A±B) = sinAcosB ± cosAsinB
cos(A±B) = cosAcosB ∓ sinAsinB
